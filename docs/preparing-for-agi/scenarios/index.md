# AGI Risk Scenarios for Australia

**Stress-test premise:** Imagine that by the mid-2030s most Australian government departments and all four major banks rely on AI systems from the same three US firms. One provider threatens to restrict access unless Australia softens proposed transparency rules, and Canberra backs down. A security researcher later discovers that the system sends telemetry data to a foreign jurisdiction, but the system is too embedded to replace quickly.

---

## Six scenarios for Australia

Each scenario illustrates a different threat pathway, grounded in Australian institutions, places and timeframes. They connect to the [C·A·G·R framework](../framework/index.md) and include questions to help you assess implications for your context.

!!! warning "These are not predictions"

    These scenarios are planning tools, not forecasts. They illustrate how different risks could emerge, helping you understand possibilities and prepare accordingly. Real events will differ in specifics while potentially following similar patterns.

> **Audience:** Government, business and community leaders | **Time:** 20–30 minutes + scenario reading

    **Timeline:** Near to medium-term · **C·A·G·R:** Resilience, Governance

    Synthetic media floods elections and public debate. Fact-checkers can't keep up. Citizens retreat into partisan bubbles where AI-generated content reinforces existing beliefs.

    **Timeline:** Near-term (underway) · **C·A·G·R:** Resilience, Governance

-   :lucide-zap:{ .lg .middle } **[Critical Infrastructure Cascade](scenario-critical-infrastructure.md)**

    ---

    AI-optimised energy, transport and communications systems work well independently — until a heatwave hits and they fail together. Manual overrides don't work. Recovery takes weeks.

    **Timeline:** Medium-term · **C·A·G·R:** All four pillars

-   :lucide-shield-alert:{ .lg .middle } **[Catastrophic Misuse](scenario-catastrophic-misuse.md)**

    ---

    Advanced AI capabilities that once required nation-state resources become accessible to smaller actors. Bioweapon design, cyber-offence and sophisticated social engineering at scale.

    **Timeline:** Medium to longer-term · **C·A·G·R:** Containment, Resilience

-   :lucide-cpu:{ .lg .middle } **[Loss of Control](scenario-loss-of-control.md)**

    ---

    **Timeline:** Longer-term (higher uncertainty) · **C·A·G·R:** Alignment, Containment, Resilience

## Not all risks require misaligned AI

!!! tip "Important distinction"

    Even if technical safety problems are fully solved, Australia still needs governance, resilience and democratic participation in AGI transformation. **Alignment is necessary but not sufficient.**

The scenarios fall into three categories:

| **Category** | **Scenarios** | **Core issue** |
|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Governance failures** | [Power Concentration](scenario-power-concentration.md), [Gradual Disempowerment](scenario-gradual-disempowerment.md), [Information Ecosystems](scenario-information-ecosystems.md) | Concentration, dependency and manipulation can occur even when systems behave as designed |
| **Safety failure** | [Loss of Control](scenario-loss-of-control.md) | Systems pursue unintended objectives or resist oversight |
| **Complex dependencies** | [Critical Infrastructure](scenario-critical-infrastructure.md), [Catastrophic Misuse](scenario-catastrophic-misuse.md) | Existing vulnerabilities become more severe as capability and access increase |

## Focus on capabilities, not definitions

AI strategy can get stuck debating the definition of "AGI". These scenarios instead focus on capabilities—what systems can actually do and who can access them.

This means preparing for a security environment where:

- **Capabilities arrive effectively "for free":** As general reasoning improves, systems gain specialised skills (cyber-offence, persuasion, bioweapon design) without explicit training for those purposes
- **Access lowers barriers:** Advanced capabilities that previously required nation-state resources become accessible to smaller actors

The practical question is not whether a system meets one AGI definition, but whether a capability changes Australia's exposure or ability to respond.

As [Joel Predd argues from work at RAND's Center for the Geopolitics of AGI](https://geopoliticsagi.substack.com/p/taking-agi-seriously-not-literally), the significance of AGI is that specific capabilities could come “for free” with more general systems—without needing a separately developed specialist AI.

---

## How scenarios connect: compounding effects

Real crises can activate several threat pathways at once:

- **Power concentration + information ecosystem strain:** Provider concentration makes democratic accountability harder during a crisis.
- **Gradual disempowerment + critical infrastructure failure:** Institutions cannot revert quickly when manual capability has atrophied.
- **Information ecosystem degradation + any scenario:** Low trust makes coordinated response harder.

**For planning:** When running exercises, deliberately combine 2-3 scenarios to test responses under compounding stress.
