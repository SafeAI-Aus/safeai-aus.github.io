# Sector Guidance for Advanced AI and AGI

This section helps Australian organisations and communities apply the [C·A·G·R framework](../preparing-for-agi/framework/index.md) to the responsibilities and decisions they own.

It focuses on strategic preparedness for advanced AI systems and possible AGI—not routine AI implementation, a prediction about when AGI will arrive or a substitute for current governance and legal advice. **Planning question:** What are we responsible for, which decisions cannot be deferred and how would we respond if AI capabilities changed rapidly?

## How to use this guidance

1. **Start with the framework.** Understand how [Containment, Alignment, Governance and Resilience](../preparing-for-agi/framework/index.md) work together as defence in depth.
2. **Choose the sector page closest to your responsibilities.** Each page translates the four pillars into decisions and areas of ownership.
3. **Use scenarios to test assumptions.** The [risk scenarios](../preparing-for-agi/scenarios/index.md) are planning tools for examining dependencies, failure pathways and response options—not predictions.
4. **Connect strategy to present-day practice.** Use SafeAI-Aus core guidance to strengthen current governance, adoption and assurance foundations.

The framework is a diagnostic lens, not a requirement to give every pillar equal attention. Each sector page emphasises the pillars where that group has the clearest responsibility or practical leverage and treats the others as supporting or indirect where appropriate.

The [timelines overview](../preparing-for-agi/timelines.md) can help teams discuss uncertainty without treating any forecast as settled.

## Two connected horizons

Advanced AI preparedness builds on good governance today, but it asks different questions.

| Horizon | Primary question | Where to go |
|---|---|---|
| **AI governance today** | How do we adopt and govern current AI systems responsibly? | [AI Standards & Legislation](../safety-standards/), [Guidance for AI Adoption (AI6)](../safety-standards/guidance-for-ai-adoption-ai6.md), [Governance Templates](../governance-templates/policy-template-library.md) and [Business Resources](../business-resources/) |
| **Advanced AI and possible AGI** | How might responsibilities, dependencies and decision thresholds change as capabilities become broader or more autonomous? | This section, the [C·A·G·R framework](../preparing-for-agi/framework/index.md) and the [risk scenarios](../preparing-for-agi/scenarios/index.md) |

These horizons overlap. Strong current governance creates the decision-making, assurance and resilience capabilities needed for more demanding futures. The sector guidance therefore links back to SafeAI-Aus core content where it provides the practical foundation, without duplicating it here.

## Scope

The guidance is concerned with advanced systems that could materially change organisational autonomy, strategic decision-making, critical dependencies or the pace and scale of risk. It does not assume that AGI will arrive on a particular date or follow a single pathway.

Across the sector pages, the structure is consistent while the C·A·G·R weighting differs:

- responsibilities through C·A·G·R;
- critical decisions and thresholds;
- relevant scenarios to exercise;
- practical steps that improve preparedness now; and
- links to current SafeAI-Aus guidance where operational detail belongs.
